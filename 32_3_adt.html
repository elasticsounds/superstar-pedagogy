Consideration 7: Time spent on technology during a lesson is tied to the tools purpose.

Learning is rarely limited to a single digital tool, but rather, it encompasses a variety of tasks that may require digital assistance.

Tip: Plan time for engagement with digital tools, but balance it with breaks to ensure rest for eyes, mind, and body.

Furthermore, always be ready to work without technology.

For example, writing with pen and paper can be combined with a digital tool, where pen and paper are used for the planning of a story, and the digital tool is used to produce it.
