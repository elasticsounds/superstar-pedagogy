Examples of digital competencies in the context of social & collaborative learning

Digital Safety

  • Understand safe identification and login processes for learners, including who has the authority to edit credentials such as real names, nicknames, pseudonyms, or photos.
  • Ensure that learners’ personal expressions are safeguarded from reaching the open internet or any broader digital social space unless explicitly necessary.

Accessibility & Inclusion

  • Perform user testing with platforms or tools prior to full implementation, ensuring compatibility with assistive technologies for social interaction.
  • Confirm that interactions can be conducted through various formats, including text, videos, and audio, to accommodate diverse needs.

Digital Work

  • Gain a thorough understanding of user interfaces (UIs) from the learner’s perspective, identifying key steps to help learners become acquainted and feel secure.
  • Determine whether the social elements of a tool require an Internet connection, or if they can be facilitated differently in areas with limited connectivity.

AI & Analytics

  • Stay informed about how AI and analytics respond to social interactions, and whether they can provide measurable and reliable data for instance on student participation in social activities.
  • Utilise AI tools for moderating social interaction content and for assisting with translations during discussions.
