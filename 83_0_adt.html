Additional information and resources

Concepts often related to digital pedagogy and educational technology

Learning Design

Learning design is the process of making informed decisions about the various elements that go into creating effective and engaging learning experiences for learners, based on educational theory and the science of how people learn. It involves choosing the content, structure, sequence, activities, strategies, assessments, and technology that support learning. An important aspect of learning design is to facilitate inclusive education by creating a Universal Design for Learning (UDL).

Maker Learning/Education

Maker education offers a transformational approach to teaching and learning that positions agency and student interest at the centre, asking students to become more aware of the design of the world around them, and to begin to see themselves as people who can tinker, hack and improve that design. Often, coding education is linked with the Maker movement, as well as the interdisciplinary views of science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics (STEAM).

Mobile Learning

Mobile learning had its peak during the last decade, but still it is evident that handheld and other evolving mobile technologies provide all the functionalities needed to perform as a device to help teachers to facilitate learning wherever the learner is. In mobile learning, the learning does not necessarily happen in the classroom or at the table either: mobile devices allow users to leverage the possibilities to take the device where the learning can happen most efficiently, such as gathering natural information outdoors. Another aspect is the use of mobile phones in areas where schooling is hard to access for learners due to catastrophic events or long distances, rendering traditional schooling is impossible.

21st Century Skills

There are several frameworks for 21st-century learning, with the main skills being: critical thinking, problem-solving, communication, creativity and innovation, collaboration, contextual learning, ICT literacy, and information management. The 21st-century skills movement emerged from the notion that more high-level cognitive skills will be needed in the digitally developed world, and that education providers should support students in cultivating a deeper level of knowledge and comprehension.
